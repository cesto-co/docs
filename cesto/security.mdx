## Non-custodial by design

Cesto is non-custodial: you own your wallet and the assets in it, and Cesto never takes custody of your funds. There's no Cesto-held account, escrow, or vault standing between you and your money.

What that means in practice depends on your wallet:

- **Embedded wallet (Google sign-in).** Cesto sets up a wallet for you using multi-party computation (MPC), powered by Para. Your private key is split into shares and is **never held or assembled in one place**: not by Cesto, not by Para. Cesto stores only an encrypted share and cannot sign on its own; every signature is produced together with Para's MPC service. You can **export your private key** or **withdraw to any wallet** at any time. The flip side of being in control: if you export your key and lose it, no one, including Cesto or Para, can recover it for you.
- **External wallet (Phantom, Solflare, …).** Your own self-custody wallet, connected directly. You hold the keys exactly as you do on any Solana app.

## You authorize every action

Cesto executes transactions for you, but only the ones you authorize. Every time you invest, rebalance, close a position, or withdraw, you sign **that specific action**: the amount, the basket, and (for withdrawals) the destination address. Cesto then carries it out exactly as signed. Nothing else moves.

Behind the scenes, the on-chain transaction is signed server-side together with Para's MPC, but only after your signed authorization for that exact action has been verified. There are no standing spending permissions: each action is authorized on its own, and authorizations are single-use and short-lived.

<Note>
**One exception, by your choice: auto-rebalance.** If you switch on auto-rebalance for a basket, you pre-authorize Cesto to realign *that* position into new versions of the strategy on your behalf, so you don't have to sign each update. It's **off by default**, scoped to that basket, and you can turn it off anytime, after which every rebalance needs your signature again. [Learn more](/cesto/rebalancing).
</Note>

## What Cesto can't do

- Move or spend your funds beyond the actions you authorize (and any auto-rebalance you've turned on)
- Hold or assemble your full private key
- Freeze or seize your assets
- Reverse a transaction once it's confirmed on-chain
- Recover your private key if you export it and lose it

## Best practices

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Secure your sign-in" icon="user-shield">
    Protect the Google account or external wallet you use to access Cesto, and turn on two-factor authentication where you can.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Guard an exported key" icon="key">
    If you export your embedded wallet's private key, or use an external wallet, never share the key or seed phrase. Cesto will never ask for it, and no legitimate service will.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Verify the URL" icon="globe">
    Always make sure you're on app.cesto.co before signing in or connecting a wallet.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Review before authorizing" icon="eye">
    Check the details of any action (amounts, assets, and destinations) before you sign.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
